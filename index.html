Cable Crunch

Sets3
Reps12 – 15
Tempo2-1-2
RPE8

Kneeling at the cable stack, rope attachment at the top, hands at the sides of the head — crunch the rib cage down toward the pelvis, not the head toward the floor. This distinction is everything. The rectus abdominis contracts by shortening the distance between the sternum and the pubic bone — not by moving the head. The cable provides consistent resistance through the entire contraction, unlike a crunch on the floor where the load disappears at the top.

This is one of the only direct resistance exercises for the rectus abdominis that allows progressive overload — you add weight as you get stronger, which is how muscles actually grow. Hold the peak contraction for one full second on every rep and feel the entire front of the abdomen fully contracted before returning.

At 103 kg, the rectus abdominis is working every session to brace the spine — it deserves to be trained directly, with load, and with the same progressive intent as any other muscle.

Chapter II.

Lower.

Tuesday · 75–85 Minutes · 6 Blocks

Built around the cleanest antagonist pairings in lower body training: quad against hamstring, calf against tibialis anterior. The squat stands alone as the primary strength block, then every subsequent pair balances the load across opposing muscle groups.

A Solo · Primary Strength
Rest 4 min between sets
A · Primary Compound Compound

Hack Squat

Sets4
Reps6 – 8
Tempo3-1-1
RPE8

The primary compound of the session. The hack squat machine fixes the torso upright and guides the path of movement — which for an athlete at 183 cm is a significant mechanical advantage. With the back supported, the quadriceps become the sole limiting factor. There is nowhere to hide and nowhere for the load to redistribute. The vastus lateralis, vastus medialis, rectus femoris, and gluteus maximus all work at maximum under a deeply controlled descent.

Feet shoulder-width on the platform, toes slightly flared. The fixed back pad eliminates spinal compression entirely — meaning you can take the quads closer to true failure than any barbell squat variation safely allows at your bodyweight.

For a 103 kg athlete, removing axial load from the primary quad movement and replacing it with machine-guided resistance is not a compromise — it is intelligent programming.

Push.

Chapter IV.

Pull.

Thursday · 85–95 Minutes · 7 Blocks

This session is built to leave no pulling muscle untouched. Every portion of the back — width, thickness, upper, middle, and lower — is addressed through a distinct movement pattern. The rear shoulder and rotator cuff receive dedicated isolation. The bicep is trained at both the stretched and contracted position. The forearms and grip are worked directly. The upper trap is fully elevated. Seven blocks, every pulling muscle covered.

A Solo · Primary Vertical Pull
Rest 3 min between sets
A · Back Width and Lower Trap Compound

Wide-Grip Lat Pulldown

Sets4
Reps6 – 8
Tempo3-1-2
RPE8

The session opens with the primary vertical pull — the movement pattern that builds the width of the back from the armpit down to the hip. A grip wider than shoulder width increases the stretch on the back width muscle at the top and shifts emphasis away from the bicep, forcing the back to initiate and drive every rep. Pull the bar to the upper chest, elbows driving downward and slightly forward.

The three-second eccentric is mandatory. The fully lengthened position at the top of every rep — where the arms are extended overhead — is where the back width muscle is stretched most completely, and that stretched position under load is where the hypertrophic stimulus is strongest. Do not rush through it. At the bottom, squeeze the shoulder blades down and back hard before initiating the next rep.

This also trains the lower trapezius — the portion of the trap that runs beneath the shoulder blade — which is rarely trained directly and which is responsible for keeping the shoulder blade properly positioned during all overhead and pulling movements.

Latissimus Dorsi·Teres Major·Lower Trapezius·Rear Deltoid·Biceps

Rack Pull (Below Knee)

Sets4
Reps4 – 6
Tempo1-1-1
RPE8–9

Bar set just below knee height in the rack. At 183 cm the conventional deadlift from the floor places the initiation point at the most mechanically disadvantaged position your limb ratios produce. The rack pull removes that disadvantage entirely, concentrating the load through the range where the lower back, entire trapezius from top to bottom, middle back, glutes, hamstrings, and forearms are at their absolute strongest. The load handled here is significantly greater than any conventional deadlift.

Engage the muscles of the back hard before the bar leaves the rack — the lats lock in, the chest lifts, the entire posterior chain fires as a unit. Drive the hips through completely at lockout and squeeze the glutes. Grip the bar until the forearms burn. This is where genuine back thickness is built — not from cable rows, not from machines, but from moving serious load through a serious range.

At 103 kg pulling from below the knee, you are in territory most people never reach. This is the most important set of the week.

Every single pulling muscle — back width, back thickness, upper trap, lower trap, mid-trap, rear shoulder, rotator cuff, both bicep heads, brachialis, brachioradialis, forearm flexors, and forearm extensors — receives its own dedicated stimulus through the exercise best suited to develop it. RPE8

Pendulum Squat

Sets4
Reps6 – 8
Tempo3-1-1
RPE8

The pendulum squat moves on a rotating arc rather than a fixed linear track — which means the resistance curve changes throughout the range of motion, loading the quads and glutes most heavily at the bottom where they are fully stretched and where the growth stimulus is highest. This is mechanically distinct from both the hack squat and the leg press, and represents a third unique quad loading pattern across the week.

The back is supported and the spine carries no axial load — strategically appropriate on Friday after rack pulls on Thursday. Feet positioned slightly higher on the platform than a hack squat to increase glute recruitment. The arc of the machine naturally keeps the torso vertical, making this one of the most joint-friendly primary compounds available for a 103 kg athlete.

The pendulum squat is considered by many strength coaches to be the superior machine squat precisely because the arc-based resistance mimics the strength curve of a free-weight squat more accurately than any fixed linear machine.
